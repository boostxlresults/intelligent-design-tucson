## Why Is It Important to Troubleshoot Common Issues During or After a Repiping Project?

Before getting into the actual troubleshooting process, it’s essential to understand why it is important to identify and address any issues arising during or after a [repiping](https://www.idesignac.com/plumber-tucson/repipe-specialist-tucson/) project. A plumbing project of this magnitude can be costly, time-consuming, and potentially disruptive to your home life.

By identifying and addressing these issues early on, you can prevent them from becoming more serious. But the benefit is not limited to just that. Here are some reasons why it’s important to troubleshoot common issues during or after a repiping project:

**Prevent water damage**: Common issues during or after a repiping project, such as leaks or faulty connections, can cause water damage to your home or business. By identifying and addressing these issues early on, you can prevent costly water damage and protect your property.

**Improve performance**: Common issues, such as clogs or low water pressure, can affect the performance of your plumbing system. By troubleshooting and addressing these issues, you can improve your plumbing system’s overall performance and functionality.

**Save money**: You can save money on repairs and maintenance in the long run by troubleshooting and fixing common issues during or after a repiping project. Ignoring these issues can lead to more serious problems that can be costly.

**Avoid Stress**: Troubleshooting issues before they become serious can help to keep stress levels low throughout the project.

## Different Pipe Types and Their Troubleshooting Tips

One of the most common issues encountered during or after a repiping project is choosing the wrong type of pipe for the job. Different types of pipes have different strengths, weaknesses, and applications, so it’s important to familiarize yourself with these before starting your project. Here are some of the piping materials and tips on how to troubleshoot different types of pipes:

### Copper Pipes

Copper pipes are a popular choice for repiping projects, as they are durable and easy to work with. If you’re having trouble with your copper pipe installation, the most common issue can be due to improper soldering.

Be sure to follow all instructions when soldering copper pipes, and make sure that the joint is completely sealed. It is also best to have a professional do the copper piping for you to ensure it will turn out right.

### PVC Pipes

PVC pipes are a good choice for repiping projects, as they are relatively inexpensive and easy to work with. However, the most common issue encountered with PVC pipe is improper gluing or sealing. Make sure that you follow all instructions when gluing PVC pipe joints together, and check for proper sealing after installation.

### PEX Pipes

PEX pipes are becoming increasingly popular for repiping projects, as they are flexible and easy to install. However, the most common issue encountered with PEX pipe is improper crimping of fittings. Be sure to follow all instructions when working with PEX pipe, and check for proper crimping after installation.

### Galvanized Steel

Galvanized steel pipes are durable and long-lasting, but they can be difficult to work with. The most common issue encountered when working with galvanized steel is corrosion. Make sure you inspect the pipe for any signs of corrosion before installation and use a sealant or primer on the pipe if necessary.

### Lead Pipes

Lead pipes are an older type of pipe and should only be used if absolutely necessary. The most common issue encountered with lead pipes is improper installation, which can lead to serious health risks. Always follow all instructions when working with lead pipes, and test the water for any signs of contamination after installation.

### CVC Pipes

CVC pipes are often used in drainage applications, as they are easy to work with and cost-effective. However, the most common issue encountered with CVC pipes is improper installation or connections. Be sure to follow all instructions when working with CVC pipe, and check for proper fitting after installation.

By familiarizing yourself with these tips, you can prevent common plumbing problems and ensure that your repiping project is a success.

## Common Repiping Issues and How to Troubleshoot Them

Now that you know why it’s important to troubleshoot potential problems during or after a repiping project, let’s look at some common issues and how to troubleshoot them.

### Leaks

If you notice a leak anywhere in your plumbing system, the first step is to identify the source of the leak and determine if an issue caused it with your repiping project or is related to an existing plumbing issue. If you notice moisture or wet spots on walls, ceilings, or floors near your plumbing fixtures, there may be a leak somewhere in your system.

Check for any visible signs of water damage and then inspect all the pipes to find the source of the leak. If all else fails, contact a professional plumber who can help you diagnose and repair the issue.

### Clogs

Clogs are a common issue in many plumbing systems. If you notice that your drains are starting to slow down or become clogged more often, it could be due to a problem with the repiping project.

Blockage causes include sediment accumulation in pipes, foreign objects clogging the lines, or even tree roots growing into the piping. You should look for any visible blockages, take action to clear them, and test the water flow in the system.

To troubleshoot this issue, you should first check for any blockages in the pipes themselves. If nothing is blocking the pipes, you may consider [cleaning your drains professionally](https://www.idesignac.com/drain-cleaning-tucson/), as this could be a more effective solution.

### Low Water Pressure

Several issues, including leaks and clogs, can cause low water pressure. To troubleshoot this issue, you should first check for any blockages or leaks in the pipes. If nothing is found, you may need to call a professional plumber to assess the system and make any necessary repairs.

### Noisy Pipes

Noisy pipes can be caused by various issues, such as air bubbles in the system or lose fittings. To troubleshoot this issue, you should check for air bubbles and ensure that all fittings are properly secured. If the noise persists, it is recommended to call a professional plumber, as there may be an underlying issue that needs to be addressed.

### Poor Plumbing Connections

Faulty or improper connections can cause a great deal of frustration and lead to problems with the performance of your system. You should check all plumbing connections for leaks, including those between pipes, fixtures, and other components. If you find any issues, it’s best to replace them with new connections that are correctly sized and secured.

### Insufficient Pressure

If you’re experiencing low pressure or no water flows through your repiped home, a blockage can cause this in one of the pipes. To quickly identify the blockage, use a water pressure gauge and check each pipe individually. If one of the pipes is blocked, you can use a drain auger to clear it out.

### Corrosion

Over time, older pipes are prone to corrosion, resulting in a decrease in water pressure and flow. This can be easily identified by looking for rusty spots on the pipes or fittings. The best way to combat corrosion is to replace the affected pipe or fitting with a new one.

### Poor Drainage

If you notice that water is not properly draining away from your home, this may be due to [clogged drain](https://www.idesignac.com/drain-cleaning-tucson/) lines. To unclog them, you can use a combination of liquid drain cleaner and a drill. You can also use a snake or auger to break up any blockages and flush them out with hot water.

By being aware of these common issues and how to quickly and efficiently troubleshoot them, you can ensure that your repiping project goes as smoothly and efficiently as possible.

## What to Look For After A Plumbing Company Has Repiped Your Home

Once the repiping project is complete, it is important to look for any red flags that may indicate a problem. While they are professionals, it is always beneficial to be aware of potential issues. If possible, don’t let the plumbers leave without checking the overall condition of your pipes and any areas that have been worked on. Here are some things to look out for before the plumbers leave:

**Check for Leaks**: Ensure all pipes’ joints are secure and free from leaks. If necessary, run water through them to check for any signs of leaking.

**Test Pressures**: To ensure proper performance, you should also test the pressure levels of each pipe. This will help you identify potential issues and ensure the system is working correctly.

**Visual Inspection**: Take a look at the piping to ensure everything looks clean and well-installed. Pay attention to joints, valves, and fittings for any signs of wear or damage.

**Check the Water Quality**: Check the quality of the water coming from your faucets to ensure it is clear and free of any unusual odors or tastes. If you notice any issues with the water quality, it may be a sign of a problem with the pipes or other components of your plumbing system.

**Test the Functionality**: Check the functionality of your plumbing fixtures, such as your faucets and toilets, to ensure they are operating properly. If you notice any issues with their performance, it may be a sign of a problem with your plumbing system’s pipes or other components.

By keeping an eye out for these issues after a plumbing company has repiped your home, you can identify and address any problems early on, which can help to prevent costly repairs and water damage.
