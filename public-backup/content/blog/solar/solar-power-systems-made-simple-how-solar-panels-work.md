## **Parts of a Solar Power System and How They Work**

A solar panel is a device that converts light into electricity. A solar panel system is made up of many solar cells that are connected. Solar cells are made of silicon, a material found in sand. When sunlight hits a solar cell, it knocks electrons loose from the atoms in the silicon. The solar cell has two layers of silicon.

One layer has extra electrons, and one layer does not have enough electrons. The solar cell has wires that connect these two layers and the solar cell creates an electric field. This field forces the electrons to flow from the side with too many electrons to the side with too few electrons. This flow of electrons creates electricity. Solar cells are usually put together into solar power systems. These systems can power homes, businesses, and even entire communities.

People install a solar panel system on their rooftops to generate electricity and save money on their energy bills. Solar panels can also generate electricity for people who live in remote areas where it is difficult to connect to the power grid.

## **Photovoltaic Effect**

The photovoltaic effect is the process solar panels use to convert sunlight into solar electricity. Solar panels are made up of solar cells, and when sunlight hits the solar cells, it causes an electrical reaction. This electrical reaction produces solar energy, which can power your home or business.

Solar panels are most commonly made from monocrystalline solar cells, which are made up of a single crystal. Monocrystalline solar cells are more efficient than other solar cells and can capture more sunlight. As a result, monocrystalline solar panels are the most popular type of solar panel on the market today.

You can substantially reduce your electric bill if you install solar panels in your home or business. Many people who have installed solar panels have seen their electric bills drop by 50% or more. The photovoltaic effect is a clean and renewable way to generate electricity, and it is becoming increasingly popular as the cost of solar panels continues to decline.
