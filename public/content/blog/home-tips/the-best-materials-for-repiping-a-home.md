## What is Repiping?

[Repiping](https://www.idesignac.com/plumber-tucson/repipe-specialist-tucson/) is a process of replacing the existing piping system for water and/or other fluids running within or outside of your home. This type of plumbing project can be required for a variety of reasons such as age, corrosion, damage from freezing temperatures, or even just to upgrade the material used.

It usually requires more time and cost than traditional plumbing repairs, but it can help protect your home from future plumbing problems.

Plumbing system repiping involves removing old pipes and replacing them with new ones. A sewer or septic system repiping job can also involve replacing the entire system from the main sewer line to the fixtures. Repiping a home is a major project and should be completed by an experienced professional.

### What is the Best Material for Repiping a Home?

When it comes to repiping a home, there are many options available. Some materials are more cost-effective and efficient than others, while others may better suit certain water or climate conditions. The three best materials for repiping a home include CPVC, copper, and PEX piping.

**CPVC**

Due to its affordability and capacity to handle high temperatures, CPVC (Chlorinated Polyvinyl Chloride) is the most popular plastic piping material, making it the ideal choice for hot water and drain lines. It is immune to corrosion, non-toxic, insulated, flameproof, energy and money-saving and capable of withstanding strong water pressure.

CPVC is a safe alternative for drinking water since it is stronger than regular PVC and contains additional chlorine. It is a good option for DIYers because it is adaptable, readily accessible in a variety of sizes, and simple to use.

But because it can deteriorate over time and become brittle, crack, or explode, it might not be suitable for repiping the entire house. Plumbing systems that use CPVC need to be regularly checked and maintained. CPVC pipes are often installed with a specialized solvent that can irritate the skin and eyes.

**Copper**

The most common and widely used piping material for plumbing purposes in homes is copper. It is the ideal replacement for iron and steel pipes due to its strength, flexibility, longevity, resistance to corrosion, and capacity to withstand heat. Copper can be recycled, needs fewer maintenance tasks, repairs, and leak fixes, and does not contaminate drinking water.

One of the best materials for repiping the entire house is this one. The price of copper is one reason that discourages homes from utilizing it. Copper pipes are expensive and require a professional plumber to install them correctly. The piping materials also come with a limited lifetime warranty which makes it an ideal choice for repiping.

**PEX (Cross-Linked Polyethylene)**

Cross-linked polyethylene, also known as PEX, is a form of plastic piping that is less expensive than copper, more lightweight, flexible than CPVC pipes, and simple to install even in confined spaces. PEX is one of the best plumbing advancements in recent history and is best suited for rebuilding or retrofitting an old house.

Due to its resistance to chlorine and scale buildup, PEX is steadily replacing copper as the most preferred piping material for new residences. It is more energy-efficient, regarded as the best material for transferring both hot and cold water, and will only require replacement for a short period.

Complete home repiping projects call for a lot of PEX pipes. Because exposure to UV rays can damage it, it is best used indoors.

These three materials are the best for repiping a home, but depending on the house’s water needs, budget, and plumbing system, homeowners can decide which one is right for them. Professional plumbers should be consulted to ensure that the job is done correctly.

### Benefits of Repiping

Repiping is an important home maintenance task that should not be overlooked. Replacing old, corroded piping with modern materials can have several benefits for your home and its occupants. Here are just a few:

*   **Improved water pressure** – Galvanized steel and other aged piping materials are prone to corrosion, which can lead to low water pressure. Newer and better-suited materials will fix this problem quickly. Copper piping is one of the most popular choices as it’s reliable and resistant to corrosion. It can also be found in flexible forms, making it easier to navigate around corners or angles.
*   **Better water quality** – Lead pipes have been used for centuries in homes to carry water, but they often corrode and decay over time. This can lead to low-quality water full of impurities that can cause health problems. Repiping a home with modern materials like copper, PEX, or PVC will ensure better water quality and reduce the risk of contamination. The quality of the water will improve, as these materials are more resistant to corrosion and decay.
*   **Decreased risk of leaks** – Plumbing fixtures and pipes can corrode and decay over time, leading to leaks. These leaks can cause flooding and damage to walls, floors, and furniture. Replacing old, weak pipes with more durable modern materials will reduce the risk of these damaging floods happening in your home.
*   **Reduced water bills** – New pipes made of PEX, copper, and CPVC can help reduce water bills. Copper pipes are the most expensive option but they last longer than other materials. They also provide the best temperature control making them ideal for those who want to keep their energy bills low. Existing pipes can be replaced with PEX, which is a great option if you’re looking for an affordable solution
*   **Peace of mind** – Repiping a house can be a daunting task, but the peace of mind that comes with knowing you have modern and reliable materials installed in your home makes it all worth it. With improved water pressure, better water quality, and reduced risk of leaks, your home will be much safer and more enjoyable to live in.

As you can see, there are several benefits to repiping your home. It’s important to choose the right materials for the job and consult with a professional plumber if you don’t have experience with plumbing projects.
