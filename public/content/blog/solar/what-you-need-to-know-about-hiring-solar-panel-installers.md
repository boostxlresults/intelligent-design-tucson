## What is a Solar Panel Installation?

Solar panel installation is the process of installing solar photovoltaic panels on the roof of a building. The panels convert sunlight into electricity for use in homes and businesses. The installations can be done professionally by a company that specializes in solar panel installation or by the homeowner.

However, it’s important to note that installing panels on your own home may not be worth the effort if you don’t have prior experience with electrical work.

The first step in this process is to determine if your roof is suitable for solar panels. If your roof cannot support them, then there’s no point in trying to install them yourself, you’ll need to hire an experienced company or individual who will help you determine whether or not your roof can support solar panels.

Once you know that your roof is suitable for solar panel installation, you’ll need to decide how many solar panels should go up there and how large they should be. This will depend on how much electricity you want from them and how much money you want to spend on them. Once again, hiring certified energy practitioners may be helpful because they will know what size and number of panels will work best for your situation.

Finally, after all those decisions are made, solar panel installation can begin. The process is fairly straightforward and should only take a few days to complete. When it’s done, you’ll be able to enjoy all the benefits that come with owning solar panels, including reduced energy costs and cleaner air!

### Types of Solar Installation

There are many different types of solar panel installations, and each one has its benefits and drawbacks. Each type of solar installation also has a different set of requirements and regulations that must be considered before you choose it. The following is a description of the most common types of solar installations:

**Roof-mount**

Roof-mounted solar panels are placed on top of your roof, which means they’re exposed to the elements and can be damaged by hail. However, they’re easy to install and don’t require any wiring work or other changes to your home. They also come in many different sizes to fit whatever space you have available on your roof.

**Dry Mount**

Dry mounting involves placing solar panels on racks that are raised slightly above ground level and tilted toward the sun so that rain doesn’t collect on them. This type of installation requires more space than other types because it involves building raised platforms for each panel, but it keeps your panels cleaner and allows them to absorb more sunlight than if they were mounted flat against the ground.

**Ground Mount**

Ground mounts are similar in appearance to dry mounts but instead sit flush with the ground rather than being elevated above it. They tend to be less expensive upfront but require more maintenance throughout their lifespans due to their exposure to dirt, moisture, and other contaminants.

**Semi-ground Mount**

Semi-ground mounts are like ground mounts, except they sit slightly above the ground instead of flush with it. This makes them harder to reach but also less susceptible to damage from wind and rain due to their elevated position.

You want to hire from one of the solar panel companies that has been around for a while and who has worked on other projects like yours before. Many solar companies offer financing options so that you can pay for your system over time instead of paying out-of-pocket right away; ask if your installer offers this!
