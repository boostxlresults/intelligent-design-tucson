## **Renewable Energy Panel Installers Can Also Install It Quickly and Efficiently**

Renewable energy is energy that is collected from renewable resources, which are naturally replenished on a human timescale, such as sunlight, wind, rain, tides, waves, and geothermal heat. Renewable energy often provides energy in four important areas: electricity generation, air and water heating/cooling, transportation, and rural (off-grid) energy services. 

Technologies that collect solar energy include solar heating, photovoltaics, solar thermal electricity, solar architecture, and artificial photosynthesis. Solar technologies are broadly characterized as either passive solar or active solar, depending on the way they collect, convert and distribute solar energy.

Passive solar techniques include orienting a building to the Sun, selecting materials with favorable thermal mass or light-dispersing properties, and designing spaces that naturally circulate air. Active solar technologies utilize mechanical or electrical devices that convert the sun’s heat or light to another form of usable energy. 

Approximately 16% of global final energy consumption comes from renewables, with 10% from traditional biomass, mainly used for heating, and 3.4% from hydroelectricity. New renewables (small hydro, modern biomass other than burning wood), geothermal power, and heat account for the remaining 3%.

At the national level, handled by national renewable energy laboratory electricity grid operators, renewable power has achieved grid parity in at least 30 nations around the world. Grid parity occurred earlier in locations with high sunlight intensities making photovoltaics economically more competitive at peak demand times than locations with lower sunlight levels where large-scale renewable power plants using other technologies are able to provide power at a lower cost per kilowatt hour than retail prices charged by utilities for electricity at other times of day.

## **You May Be Eligible for Tax Credits or Other Incentives if You Go Solar**

Solar energy is a renewable resource that can be used to produce electricity or heat. Concentrating solar power plants use mirrors to reflect and concentrate sunlight onto receivers that collect solar energy and convert it into heat.

This heat is then used to produce electricity. Solar PV or photovoltaic systems use solar cells to convert sunlight directly into electricity. Solar water heating systems use solar collectors to absorb solar radiation and transfer the resulting heat to water.

You may be able to claim a tax credit for installing solar PV or solar water heating systems. These tax credits are available for both residential and commercial installations. In addition, some utilities offer incentives for solar PV system owners who agree to sell their excess electricity back to the utility.

Check with your state’s energy office or your local utility company to see what incentives are available in your area.
